Procedures to be Followed During Natural Disasters

1. Fire Accident

All employers are obliged to assess the risk of fire in their workplace. Below listed are the procedures that needs be followed during an emergency of fire accident. Each and every staff of Datapage should have the knowledge of the below mentioned points. The correct number and type of fire extinguisher has been provided and maintained by competent engineers. It is imperative that the right fire extinguisher is deployed against a certain type of fire. Using water on an oil based fire for instance will not only endanger the person attempting to fight the fire but will also help its spread.

A fire brigade has been formed to handle any emergency situations in Datapage. Vairamurthy (HR & Administration Officer) will be leading this team. Any instances of fire in the office premises should be first escalated to him. In his absence, Nisha Rahul (Operations Director) will be the next point of contact. The following staff form the fire brigade:

  • Vairamurthy (HR & Administration Officer)
  • Nisha Rahul (Operations Director)
  • Baranidharan (Accountant)
  • Chitra S (Account Manager - Journals)
  • Ramnath N (Deputy Manager – Journals)
  • Srikanth G (Account Manager – Books & Projects)
1.1. Fire Emergency Procedure
  • If a fire is suspected, investigate at once but do not take risks
  • If a fire is discovered - stay calm
  • Escalate the disaster to the fire brigade
  • On hearing the alarm, stop working and ensure that everything is left safe
  • Direct all other people away from the affected area
  • If it is safe to do so, try to control the fire - with help if necessary - but do not take risk
  • It may be possible to deal with small fires using the appropriate fire extinguisher
  • In the case of a fire involving electrical equipment isolate the equipment if possible before attempting to extinguish it
  • When the Fire Brigade arrives, immediately inform the Fire Officer of any special hazards, such as gas cylinders, dangerous chemicals etc. within the area affected
  • Leave the building by the nearest available exit, closing all windows, doors 
  • Do not use lifts when vacating the building
  • Evacuate the building as quickly as possible
  • Check for the presence of known colleagues
  • Do not re-enter the building until the alarm is silenced and you are authorised by a fire officer

 

2. Storm and Flooding

Unlike other natural disasters, storm and floods can be predicted beforehand. Hence, precautionary measures and recovery plans can be thought of well in advance before the actual strike of the disaster. The following are the procedures that should be followed before and after a storm or floods.

2.2 Storm and Flood Emergency Procedures
  • The DR team should be in a red alert state
  • The DC generator should be filled in with fuel and buffer fuel to be stored to handle power shortages
  • Notify municipal and state officials of our activity, in case they have special concerns
  • Alert all employees, so they'll be ready to respond to any adverse situations
  • Secure your area and vital records
  • Prepare to receive and comply with directions from Emergency Personnel.
  • Use Extreme Caution around appliances or outlets near the leak and/or water.
  • If the source of the water is known and can safely stop it, do so cautiously.
  • If directed to evacuate, do so immediately

 

3. Earthquake

Earthquakes strike without warning so it is important that a specific procedure is followed during and after its strike to minimise its impact. When an earthquake occurs, the solid earth may pitch and roll like the deck of a ship for several minutes.  Earthquakes strike without warning and there will be only seconds to react. Most casualties result from falling debris and objects, splintering window glass, and fire.  The chances of being injured during an earthquake will be reduced if the following procedures are adhered to during the earthquake.

3.1 Earthquake Emergency Procedures
  • When an earthquake is felt, duck under a sturdy table or desk
  • Stay away from windows, bookcases, file cabinets, tall furniture, heavy mirrors, hanging plants, and other heavy objects that could fall
  • Watch out for falling plaster or ceiling tiles.
  • Stay under cover until the shaking stops
  • If no table or desk is available, seek cover against an interior wall and protect your head and neck with your arms.
  • Hold onto the table or desk
  • If it moves, move with it.  Hold the position until the ground stops shaking and it is safe to move
  • Do not use elevators
  • Do not be surprised if the fire alarm sounds or sprinkler systems come on
  • Remain under cover for a few minutes and prepare for the possibility of after-shocks
  • If the building appears to have significant damage evacuate to a safe location, until emergency personnel advises you of the ability to return to your work location
  • Replace telephone receivers back on the telephone cradle
  • Check your work area for injured employees and give first aid as may be deemed appropriate
  • Seek medical treatment as may be required
  • Do not attempt to move injured persons unless they are in immediate danger
  • Check for fire hazards
  • Do not smoke or light matches
  • Do not turn on lights or other electrical circuits until you are certain that there is no risk of gas leaks
  • Remain calm and assess your situation carefully

 

4. Evacuation Procedures

The following are the general evacuation procedures that should be followed when ordered to evacuate the building during disasters.

  • If safe, secure vital records and shut down electrical equipment
  • Proceed to your alternate exit if necessary
  • Shut all doors as you leave
  • Floor staff should ensure that all staff and visitors evacuate the area
  • Assist disabled employees or visitors
  • Proceed quietly and orderly
  • Do not use elevators
  • Do not open door if hot or if smoke is present
  • Once outside, assemble at a designated area and stay there; your supervisors will need to have a tally of their personnel

It is very likely that evacuations will involve disabled individuals. The following will be helpful in safe evacuation and communication during an emergency.

Persons having mobility impairments may or may not use wheelchairs. Those individuals having mo­bility impairment, who can ambulate in varying degrees, will need to be carried down stairs, or other­wise be assisted. Ask her/him for instructions. Always consult wheelchair users about:

  • Transferring her/him from the wheelchair (or, if the emergency dictates, moving the person down stairs while staying in the wheelchair)
  • Never carry an individual backwards down a flight of stairs
  • The number of people needed for assistance
  • Be aware that some wheelchair users use catheter leg bags, braces, oxygen, prosthetics, etc.

1. Introduction

This document proposes disaster recovery and business continuity plans to address various types of possible disaster scenarios. The plans reflect the analysis and determination of appropriate responses as agreed in discussions with IT, administration, and finance departments.

The objectives of a disaster recovery plan for information services are to make sufficient preparations, and to establish a sufficient set of agreed upon procedures, for responding to a disaster or emergency, in order to minimize the effect upon the operation of the business and the time span for the resumption of the business processes.

Three areas need to be thoroughly reviewed to study the possible disasters and to have an effective recovery plan. They are:

 

• Administration – The administration department has the responsibility to protect its corporate resources and information in case of a disaster.

 

• Finance – In the event of a disaster, a great financial loss to the company could develop, or even destroy the business if proper disaster planning has not been done.

 

• Information Technology (IT) – Any major breakdown of the information services could result in a sudden halt of the business processes. This can be very damaging to future relationships with customers as there would be communication gaps. It can also affect the public image of the organization. The costs of not taking precautions could be much more damaging and costlier than modest preparation for disaster recovery.

 

2. Administration – Recovery Plans

 

The administration of any business-processing unit holds the sole responsibility to protect its corporate resources and information such as the premises, computers and staff in case of a natural or manmade disaster. Datapage administration department has already plans in place to tackle major disasters that may occur during the progress of the business processes. Discussed below are the precautionary plans and the possible disasters which could be tackled by virtue of the existence of these plans. Also listed are the suggestions for recovery plans which could be implemented in order to make the business process run in a smooth manner even when struck by disasters.

 

2.1. Insurance Coverage – Office Protection Shield Policy

The administration has insured the resources of the company under this policy. The details of the clauses covered under the policy are as follows:

 

Fire and Perils – This clause covers damages caused by the impact of natural perils such as fire, flood, earthquake, water perils, Tsunami. The entire floor and all the contents are covered under this policy. In case of an accident in the business premises due to the above natural perils, any damage to the resources and contents of the company such as systems, servers, furniture, fittings, etc. will be compensated by the insurance company if a claim is raised under the “Fire and Perils” clause coverage. The insurance company would reimburse the claim within 7–10 working days.

 

Burglary and Dacoity – This clause covers damages caused by burglary (forcible entry & exit), housebreaking and dacoity. In case of burglary or dacoity in the business premises, the loss incurred will be paid by the insurance company if a claim is raised under the “Burglary and Dacoity” clause coverage. The insurance company would reimburse the claim within 7–10 working days.

 

Additional expenditure of rent for alternative accommodation – In the event of an admissible claim, the additional rent incurred towards an alternative office is covered in case there is any major damage to the site. In case of natural disasters like earthquake, circumstances of a total shutdown of the working onsite premises may occur. In such instances, on an admissible claim, the insurance company will be bearing the additional rent incurred towards the setting up of an alternative office premises. The claim is payable up to a maximum period of 12 months or the actual period, whichever is less.

 

Claims are handled directly by the insurance company and the normal procedure has to be followed for claim processing. Normally the claims are paid in a period of 7–10 working days from the submission of all the relevant papers, such as claim forms, police reports, loss assessment reports and quotations for the processing of claim. The resumption of business operations should be wholly managed by the insured. Business operations can be resumed as soon as we arrange a new office and purchase the relevant goods. All the above clauses are on a 24x7 days coverage, renewable on a yearly basis.

 

2.2. Fire Alarm and Extinguishers

Fire Alarm – The administration has a set up of fire alarm installed in the office premises. The device is a perfect functioning and active 24x7 days. The device has three hooters installed at three places in the premises which would produce high dB sound in case of fire. The main panel which has been installed in the front office is being monitored and maintained every fortnight. A register, which holds the details of the date of inspection, the inspector’s name and his remarks, is being maintained by the security personnel. The fire alarms have also been covered under AMC.

In case of a fire accident in the business premises, the fire alarms would start buzzing. This would alert the staff and other people in the premises regarding the fire accident. The staff could be shifted to a safe area away from the fire. The alert would also initiate the next set of actions to be performed like locating the fire and putting it off.

 

Fire Extinguishers – The immediate solution to put off and control the fire in the office premises would be the use of fire extinguishers. There are totally eight fire extinguishers present in the office premises. To be more specific, a fire extinguisher has been set up for every 825 sq ft of area. Six out of the eight extinguishers are of ABC (powder type) model which can be used for putting off fire anywhere except on delicate electronic goods such as systems, servers etc, as the goods may be unusable after the exposure to the powder. The other two extinguishers are of CO2 (gas type) model which can be effectively used on electronic goods. The use of the correct model of the fire extinguishers at the correct moment would save loss of life and resources. All the fire extinguishers conform to the BSI standards. Well-trained security personnel, who can use any model fire extinguisher, are available on a 24x7 basis in the premises. Any fire at any location of the premises could be controlled effectively with the current setup.

 

2.3. Security Policies

Security Personnel – The building and the office premises are both guarded on a 24x7 basis by well-trained security personnel, who restrict the entry of any unauthorised person into the building and office premises. The security personnel check the outgoing and incoming material and keep a log in order to avoid any kind of theft or burglary. The security personnel have been provided with the escalation procedures and a telephone directory with all relevant phone numbers in order to handle an emergency situation by contacting the right person at the right time. He is also given the access to the common key vault containing the duplicate keys to different cabins, so as to deal with any disaster occurring inside a locked cabin.

 

Biometric Access Control System – The entry into the business premises is also restricted by the Biometric Access Control System installed at the front door of the office. The system would recognise only authorised thumb impressions which have already been fed as templates into the database. The access through this system would be possible only by the recognition of the thumb impression by the system. As a second level protection layer, any unauthorised entry to the production floor could be prevented by the Biometric Access Control System. This could greatly avoid burglary and dacoity.

 

Visitors Monitoring Register – A register for visitors is being maintained at the front office by the security personnel. This log will have the entry, exit and address details of the visitors. In case of any mishaps by visitors, the details of the visitor could easily be traced by looking into the details in the visitors monitoring register.

 

2.4. Evacuation Process

Emergency Exit – The office premises has an emergency exit other than the main entrance. This exit is very close to the staircase leading the occupants out of the building. In case of emergency, the emergency exit could be used as an alternate to the main entrance.

 

2.5. Power Backup

We have a special set up where one of these UPS has been dedicated for critical systems, server, access control, which works in ‘hot-standby’ mode. The response time from the AMC office is 1 hour from the time of launch of the complaint. With the help of these 2 UPS set up, any type of power failure could be easily handled for at least 1 hour.

4.3. Data Backup Procedures

One of the effective ways to tackle disasters in IT is by planning efficient data backup procedures. The major threat to the IT department is the loss of data during disasters. This threat could be overcome by having powerful backup procedures in place. The following are the backup procedures followed by Datapage:

 

Dedicated Backup Server – A dedicated backup server has been set up to replicate and backup data from the working server. This works on a 24-hour delay basis. The data from the working server would be replicated and copied to the backup server every night.

 

Storage Tapes – The data on the working server is stored into cartridge tapes on a weekly basis. This serves as a second level backup. At any point of time, data modified before a weeks’ time will be present in the tapes.

 

Raid5 Technique – One of the powerful techniques for data backup being used worldwide is the Raid5 technique. The data parity via Raid5 on the working server helps greatly during hard disk failure of the main server. Raid5 works on the parity and stripping principle. Data on the server will be stored in four different drives and a pointer pointing to the addresses of these storage locations would be present in all the drives. In case of failure of one of the drives, the data stored in that drive would be recovered from the other three working drives with the help of the pointer. Hence, the data recovery in case of emergencies would be very quick and authentic.

 

Periodic Backup Practice – There is a practice of periodic backup of important information such as application source code, duplicated data, application CDs, and license information. The vital information can hence be retrieved from the backup tapes in case of emergencies.

 

E-mail Server Backup – A procedure to backup the settings, rules and accounts of the email server is in place. The backup is done on a different server and not in the same email server. In case of any emergencies or failure of the email server, the backup email server immediately works as an alternate solution as it is the exact replica of the email server.

 

Periodic Financial Data Backup – A periodic backup procedure has been put in place for financial data. Any financial data residing in the Finance Manager’s system is being periodically backed up into CDs/DVDs.

 

Four Server Backup – The data from the working server is being backed up to four backup servers. Hence, a pool of four servers is functioning as backups for the main server during downtimes.

 

Parallel Web and Database Server on Standby – The web and the database servers have a parallel standby facility. This would greatly minimise the downtime of these servers and allow quicker redundancy during crisis.

A firewall has been set up in our network which will protect the network from virus threats, worm threats and intrusions. This acts as a first level protection layer of the network. This plays a very important role in minimising virus and worm threats to our network. The log created by the firewall is regularly monitored to identify any unauthorised network intrusion.

 

Control Access Router – This router is responsible for routing data across networks and is controlled by access policies. This enables to have a controlled access to the network. The access can be controlled based on IP address, port or both. Hence the router will not allow any unauthorised entry or exit in the network.

 

System-level Virus Protection – A system-level virus protection has been provided to all systems and servers in the network. This is known as Desktop Protection. Up-to-date Automatic Security Patching – Patching is a fix to a program bug. A patch is an actual piece of object code that is inserted into an executable program. Patches typically are available as downloads over the Internet. An up-to-date automatic patching process has been set up which would protect the systems and network from vulnerability.

 

Network Log Monitoring – The network log is being periodically monitored. Any unauthorised data entry or exit, unauthorised network usage, etc. could be identified in this process.

  • If a fire is suspected, investigate at once but do not take risks
  • On hearing the fire alarm, stop working and ensure that everything is left safe
  • Stay calm and escalate the disaster to the EFT
  • EFT can either refer the emergency directory and inform fire station immediately, or run down to the fire station office situated right in front of our building to alert them

Earthquake ­– Earthquakes strike without warning and there will be only seconds to react, so it is important that a specific procedure is followed during and after its strike to minimise its impact. When an earthquake occurs, the solid earth may pitch and roll like the deck of a ship for several minutes. Most casualties result from falling debris and objects, splintering window glass, and fire. The chances of being injured during an earthquake will be reduced if the following procedures are adhered to during the earthquake.

•    When an earthquake is felt, duck under a sturdy table or desk

•    Stay away from windows, bookcases, file cabinets, tall furniture, heavy mirrors, hanging plants, and other heavy objects that could fall

•    Watch out for falling plaster or ceiling tiles

•    Stay under cover until the shaking stops

•    If no table or desk is available, seek cover against an interior wall and protect your head and neck with your arms.

•    Hold onto the table or desk

•    If it moves, move with it.  Hold the position until the ground stops shaking and it is safe to move

•    Do not use elevators

•    Do not panic if the fire alarm sounds or sprinkler systems come on

•    Remain under cover for a few minutes and prepare for the possibility of after-shocks

•    If the building appears to have significant damage, evacuate to a safe location, until emergency personnel advise you of the ability to return to your work location

•    Replace telephone receivers back on the telephone cradle

•    Check your work area for injured employees and give first aid or medical treatment as deemed appropriate

•    Do not attempt to move injured persons unless they are in immediate danger

•    Check for fire hazards

•    Do not smoke or light matches

•    Do not turn on lights or other electrical circuits until you are certain that there is no risk of gas leaks

•    Remain calm and assess your situation carefully

 

Evacuation Procedures – The following are the general evacuation procedures that should be followed when ordered to evacuate the building during disasters.

 

•    If safe, secure vital records and shut down electrical equipment

•    Proceed to your alternate exit if necessary

•    Shut all doors as you leave

•    Floor staff should ensure that all staff and visitors evacuate the area

•    Assist disabled employees or visitors

•    Proceed quietly and orderly

•    Do not use elevators

•    Do not open door if hot or if smoke is present

•    Once outside, assemble at a designated area and stay there; the department leaders will need to have a tally of their personnel

 

It is very likely that evacuations will involve disabled individuals. Persons having mobility impairments may or may not use wheelchairs. Those individuals having mo­bility impairment, who can ambulate in varying degrees, will need to be carried down stairs, or other­wise be assisted. Ask her/him for instructions. Always consult wheelchair users about:

•    Transferring her/him from the wheelchair (or, if the emergency dictates, moving the person down stairs while staying in the wheelchair)

•    Never carry an individual backwards down a flight of stairs

•    The number of people needed for assistance

•    Be aware that some wheelchair users use catheter leg bags, braces, oxygen, prosthetics, etc.
